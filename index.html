CREATE Lab Explorables provide interactive, visual representations of large data sets that allow users to delve into the information and more fully grasp its meaning. Data in its raw form is often meaningless to the majority of people. This limits access to information that has the potential to empower communities and transform lives. We aim to make big data more accessible to the everyday citizen, as well as provide scientists with powerful tools for visualizing and communicating their research findings.

Explorables provide interactive and visual representations of large data sets, revealing patterns, encouraging discovery, and facilitating communication. The Explorables Collaborative, including CREATE Lab and SkyTruth, is dedicated to helping you make your information more impactful. Contact us!

Speck Air Quality Test
Visualization of a Speck test showing how indoor air quality is effected by vacuuming, running an air purifier, and blowing out candles.
FIRMS Timelapse
Visualization of thirteen years of MODIS fire location vector data overlayed on an explorable global timelapse.
Suburban Sprawl
Video exploring the growth of suburban sprawl in Chesterfield County, Virginia using TimeMachine to show the full scope of changes.
AirNow
Explorable visualization of AirNow PM 2.5 data showing changes in particle values and air quality across the United States.
Drilling
Time series visualization of over one million natural resource extraction wells in eight states spanning decades.
Fracking Earthquakes
Visualization of Landsat and USGS data, utilizing TimeMachine to explore the possible connection in Arkansas between earthquakes and natural gas drilling.
EVA & LEHD
Multi-dimensional visualization of LEHD (Longitudinal Employer-Household Dynamics) data using EVA (Explorable Visual Analytics).
Timelapse Story Telling
Visualization exploring landscape changes along Taiwan's coastline over two decades using TimeMachine.
Speck In-Home Test
Visualization of air quality data collected by 64 Specks located in homes around the Pittsburgh area for a period of five days.
